Activities

    Liveaboard Diving

    Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park is one of the best diving spots in the Philippines. The clear waters are home to a vast range of marine species, including rare fish, coral gardens, and sea turtles. A liveaboard dive offers an immersive experience as divers explore the stunning underwater world. Dive into pristine reefs and experience the wonder of underwater life with expert guides and divers. The Tubbataha Reefs are a UNESCO World Heritage site, and liveaboard diving here allows you to witness its unmatched beauty.

    Visit the Ranger Station

    The Tubbataha Ranger Station, located on the reef, serves as the headquarters for the conservation efforts in the park. Visitors can take guided tours to learn about the vital conservation work being done to protect the marine environment. The station also serves as a base for research activities, and it’s a great spot to learn about the park's biodiversity. You can also observe the dedicated park rangers in action, helping to preserve this natural wonder.

    Flying a Drone

    Tubbataha Reefs offers an incredible opportunity to capture stunning aerial footage. Flying a drone over the vast coral reefs provides breathtaking views of the clear blue waters, atolls, and the wildlife below. Its a unique experience that gives you a new perspective on this UNESCO World Heritage site. However, make sure to follow all the necessary regulations to ensure that drone usage doesn't interfere with conservation efforts.

    Birdwatching

    Tubbataha is also known for its remarkable birdlife, attracting birdwatchers from around the world. The park is home to various seabird species, including the endangered Brown Booby and the Black Noddy. Visitors can enjoy birdwatching during their stay, especially during the nesting season. The serene environment and the abundance of birds make it an unforgettable experience for nature enthusiasts.

    Engage in Scientific Research

    For those with an interest in marine biology and conservation, Tubbataha offers a unique opportunity to engage in scientific research. The reef is a hub for various studies related to marine ecosystems, fish populations, and coral health. Researchers and tourists alike can participate in conservation efforts, learning firsthand how Tubbatahas biodiversity is being preserved. It’s an enriching experience that contributes to understanding and protecting one of the world's most pristine marine environments.
